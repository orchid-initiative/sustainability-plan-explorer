Southern California is arid, and long-term water resilience means using less
and capturing more of what falls. Stormwater matters both ways: it can carry
pollutants straight into local waterways, or — with the right infrastructure —
refill the aquifer we all drink from. This section commits Alhambra to both
sides of that equation.

**Local wins so far:** Safe Clean Water Program projects are underway at
Burke Heritage Park and Marengo Yard — improvements that filter pollutants
and upgrade public spaces at the same time. The Main Street Green Street
Demonstration Project is turning a busy arterial median into live stormwater
infrastructure. Advanced Metering Infrastructure (AMI) is rolling out
citywide: smart water meters with real-time data and early leak detection.

**Community spotlight — Main Street Green Street:** From Fremont to Hampden
Terrace, the center median is being transformed into bioretention areas and
drywells that catch stormwater before it flushes pollutants into local
waterways — while native plantings reduce heat and enhance the streetscape.
