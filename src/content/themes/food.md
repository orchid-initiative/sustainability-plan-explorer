Our food system is a quiet sustainability lever. Traditional farming uses 70%
of the world's water, and produce travels an average of 1,500 miles to reach a
plate. Local food production and reduced waste save water, cut emissions, and
strengthen food security for every household in Alhambra.

**Local wins so far:** Alhambra's 2021 Mandatory Organic Waste Ordinance
requires every home and business to participate in organics collection.
Republic Services gives every single-family customer a food-scrap container.
Since 2022, "edible food generators" must donate surplus food to recovery
organizations.

**Community spotlight — Winston Smoyer Memorial Community Garden:** A 2.5-acre
garden at Granada and Mission Road supporting 88 plots since 1976, named one
of LA's top 10 community gardens. Annual fees run $9–$77; plot holders grow
everything from chilies to pineapple sage.
