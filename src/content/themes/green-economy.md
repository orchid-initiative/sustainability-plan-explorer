Alhambra's economic health is bound up with its environmental and social
health. This sector's premise: as the city supports new and existing
businesses and attracts quality jobs, the same moves can also build the
physical and community infrastructure residents need. That's the "triple
bottom line" — environment, economy, people — at the core of how Alhambra
thinks about economic development.

**Local wins so far:** The Green Business Program is under development in
partnership with the Chamber of Commerce, offering certification through the
California Green Business Network. Active small-business support programs are
already in place, and the City coordinates with neighboring cities on
consistent sustainability practices for businesses across the region.

**Community spotlight — Alhambra Certified Farmers Market:** Every Sunday on
South Second Street, with CalFresh Market Match (doubles purchasing power up
to $15/visit), WIC, and Senior Farmers' Market Nutrition Program support —
healthy, locally-grown food accessible across all income levels.
