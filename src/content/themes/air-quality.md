Air quality is measured by pollutant concentration and the hazards they pose
to human health. Alhambra is in the South Coast air basin, monitored by the
South Coast Air Quality Management District. The goals here focus on ozone,
PM2.5, and diesel particulate matter.

Alhambra's pollution burden percentile is 60.8 — above the LA County average
of 50. That means higher exposure to pollutants than the typical county
neighborhood. This sector is about changing that.

**Local wins so far:** 56 community-owned Purple Air sensors are now
installed citywide, giving real-time hyperlocal air-quality data. The City
partners with local health organizations on emergency response planning for
high-pollution events. The USC–APIFM Water Equity Partnership brings
community-based research on water quality and environmental health to
disadvantaged neighborhoods.

**Community spotlight — Purple Air Sensor Network:** Strategically placed in
higher-density neighborhoods and near greening projects, the sensors protect
vulnerable residents — the 18% of the population over 65, children, and
those with respiratory conditions. On track to grow from 56 to 106+ sensors
by 2030.
