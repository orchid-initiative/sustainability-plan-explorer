Alhambra's parks, tree canopy, and green infrastructure do a lot of work at once:
they clean the air, shade hot streets, catch stormwater, and give neighbors
somewhere to meet. Today the tree canopy covers about 14% of the city — this
section maps out how to roughly double that by 2045, with a focus on the
neighborhoods that need shade most.

**Local wins so far:** The City already requires drought-tolerant landscaping
on new development, enforces a 2:1 replacement rule when trees come down, and
has broken ground on the Main Street Green Street project — bioswales in the
median that capture stormwater while the new trees grow in.

**Community spotlight — Almansor Park:** A former 1950s landfill transformed
into Alhambra's largest green space, 22 acres of stormwater management, habitat
restoration, a lake, and sports facilities.
